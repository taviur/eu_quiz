Question explanations



Q1. Moving to another EU country e.g. for work or study


The government would need to negotiate factors for British expatriates living in other EU countries such as pension rights, healthcare rights and claiming benefits. Source


Leaving the EU could also effect free movement for UK citizens throughout the EU, perhaps requiring UK citizens to acquire a study or work visa before moving to a country in the EU. Source



Q2. Visiting another EU country e.g. to see family abroad or for a holiday


A significant piece of European legislation, the Denied Boarding Regulation, allows passengers to claim up to €600 in compensation for delays or cancellations on flights that originate in the EU. UK citizens travelling to the EU, should the UK leave the EU, may find it harder to hold airlines to account should anything happen to their flight. Source



Q3. Clean beaches and/or public access to information about the environment


EU regulations on water quality have been instrumental in cleaning up Britain’s beaches. Nearly 600 coastal bathing areas are monitored for potentially dangerous bacteria such as E.coli – with minimum standards set out for safe bathing which have to be displayed. If the UK leaves the EU, the UK may not hold to such regulations. Source



Q4. Workers rights e.g. maternity leave, paternity leave, holiday pay


Many rights accumulated over decades, including paid annual leave, time off for antenatal appointments and fair treatment for part-time workers, are “used every day by millions of workers”, the TUC said. But if the UK votes to leave the EU, “no one can say what will happen to these rights”. Source



Q5. Farming subsidies (when governments give money to farmers to supplement their income)


A report commissioned by the Yorkshire Agricultural Society acknowledged it was possible the UK could continue subsidy payments at existing levels, using money currently going to EU coffers, but in all likelihood the Treasury would want to reduce its overall expenditure by cutting direct payments. Source
